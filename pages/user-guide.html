User Guide

Everything you need to buy, sell, and stay safe on UniMart.

How to Buy
  1. Browse Listings: Explore available items in the Marketplace and use category or keyword filters.
  2. Review Details: Open the listing to check images, item condition, and seller-provided notes.
  3. Favorite Items: Save interesting products to My Favorites for quick access later.
  4. Contact the Seller: Use listed contact details and agree on pickup time and location.
  5. Complete Payment Safely: Verify item condition first, then complete payment using trusted methods.
  6. Confirm Completion: Once done, keep records of chats and transfer details for your protection.
How to Sell
  1. Create a clear listing title and choose the right category.
  2. Upload clean photos with good lighting and honest angles.
  3. Set a fair price and provide concise condition details.
  4. Respond promptly and agree on pickup in public campus areas.
  5. Mark sold items quickly to keep the marketplace fresh.
Refund Process
  1. Discuss issues with the seller first within 24 hours of handover.
  2. Collect evidence (photos, listing screenshots, and chat logs).
  3. Submit a report in admin channels for dispute mediation.
  4. Follow case updates and provide additional proof if requested.
Marketplace Policies
  • No prohibited, illegal, or unsafe items.
  • No spam, duplicate listings, or deceptive descriptions.
  • Respectful communication is required at all times.
  • Violations can result in listing removal or account restrictions.
About UniMart

UniMart is a student-centered marketplace for the CUHK-SZ community. It helps students buy and sell useful items quickly, sustainably, and safely.

Notifications & Updates

Watch for policy updates, security advisories, and product announcements. High-priority alerts are shown in-app and should be reviewed promptly.

Quick Tips for Success

  • Use descriptive titles and clear pricing.
  • Meet in visible, public campus areas.
  • Keep all communication polite and traceable.
  • Take screenshots for high-value transactions.
